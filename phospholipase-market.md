<p><p>The phospholipase market comprises four key types: Phospholipase A, which hydrolyzes fatty acids from the glycerol backbone; Phospholipase B, involved in the degradation of phospholipids; Phospholipase C, which cleaves phospholipids to produce inositol phosphates and diacylglycerol; and Phospholipase D, which hydrolyzes phosphatidylcholine to produce phosphatidic acid. Each type has distinct applications in biochemistry, pharmaceuticals, and food processing, driving their demand across various industries due to their roles in cellular signaling and membrane dynamics.</p></p>
