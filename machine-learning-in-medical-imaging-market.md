<p><p>The machine learning in medical imaging market comprises several types, each serving distinct purposes. Supervised learning involves training models with labeled data to classify and diagnose conditions. Unsupervised learning identifies patterns in unlabeled data, useful for anomaly detection. Semi-supervised learning combines both labeled and unlabeled data, enhancing model accuracy with limited annotated samples. Reinforcement learning optimizes decision-making models through trial and error, improving diagnostic strategies over time. Together, these approaches advance diagnostic accuracy and efficiency in medical imaging applications.</p></p>
