<p><p>The microfluidic glass market comprises primarily two types: quartz glass and borosilicate glass. Quartz glass is favored for its excellent optical properties, chemical resistance, and thermal stability, making it ideal for high-precision applications. In contrast, borosilicate glass is valued for its lower cost and good chemical durability, commonly used in various microfluidic devices. Both types are crucial in fields like biomedical research, diagnostics, and chemical analysis, enabling precise fluid manipulation at the microscale.</p></p>
