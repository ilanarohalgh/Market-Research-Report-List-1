<p><p>The pediatric upper limb orthoses market is primarily divided into dynamic and static orthotics. Dynamic orthotics are designed to facilitate movement while providing support, allowing for functional mobility and muscle engagement. They are often used in therapy for conditions like cerebral palsy or muscle weakness. In contrast, static orthotics immobilize a limb to promote healing, maintain proper alignment, or prevent contractures. Both types are crucial in addressing the specific needs of children with upper limb disabilities or injuries.</p></p>
