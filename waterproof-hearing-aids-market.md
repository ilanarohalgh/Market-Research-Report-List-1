<p><p>The waterproof hearing aids market offers two primary types: behind-the-ear (BTE) and in-the-ear (ITE) hearing aids. BTE hearing aids are worn on the outer ear, connected to an ear mold that sits in the ear canal, providing durability and enhanced moisture resistance. ITE hearing aids are custom-fit directly into the ear canal, offering discretion and comfort. Both types are designed to withstand exposure to water, sweat, and humidity, making them ideal for active users and those in humid environments.</p></p>
