<p><p>The scintillator market comprises three main types: solid, liquid, and gaseous scintillators. Solid scintillators, such as inorganic crystals or plastics, are widely used for their durability and efficiency in detecting radiation. Liquid scintillators offer high light output and are suitable for various applications like neutron detection. Gaseous scintillators, often utilized in specialized detectors, operate in low-pressure environments and are beneficial for tracking fast particles. Each type serves distinct applications in fields like nuclear physics, medical imaging, and radiation monitoring.</p></p>
