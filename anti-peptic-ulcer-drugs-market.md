<p><ul><li>Proton Pump Inhibitors (PPIs)</li><li>Potassium-Competitive Acid Blockers (P-CAB)</li><li>Antacids</li><li>H2 Antagonists</li><li>Antibiotics</li><li>Ulcer Protective Drugs</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The anti-peptic ulcer drugs market includes several key types targeting gastric acid secretion and ulcer protection. Proton Pump Inhibitors (PPIs) block proton pumps, reducing acid production. Potassium-Competitive Acid Blockers (P-CAB) offer a newer approach by inhibiting acid secretion more selectively. Antacids neutralize stomach acid for quick relief, while H2 Antagonists reduce acid production by blocking histamine receptors. Antibiotics are prescribed to eradicate H. pylori infections, and Ulcer Protective Drugs help to coat the stomach lining, promoting healing and protection.</p></p>
